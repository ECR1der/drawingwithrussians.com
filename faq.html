Frequently Asked Questions

What is Drawing with Russians?
Drawing with Russians is a non-instructed figure drawing salon loosely affiliated with Russian speakers, followed by an after-party.
When is Drawing with Russians?
Drawing with Russians is on the first Thursday of every month, except when otherwise.
Why is this event called "Drawing with Russians"?
The host coined the name in 2015 while attending a figure drawing gathering almost exclusively composed of Russian speakers, and organized by another Russian-speaking artist.

When is the next drawing?
Please subscribe to our mailing list and we'll send you an invitation about a week in advance.
What happens at figure drawing with Russians during drawing?
We chit-chat, have a drink, and draw a model in 20 minute intervals for about 2 hours.
Can I come to Drawing with Russians even though I am not Russian?
While encouraged, you do not have to be Russian or a Russian-speaker to come. Typically, 2/3 of the attendees are not Russian, but we would be happy to recommend some Russian literature for you to familiarize yourself with before your first visit.
Do I need a referral to come to Drawing with Russians?
Yes, we grow exclusively through word of mouth. Please tell us who referred you when subscribing to the list.
I am a terrible n00b at drawing. Should I still come?
Yes enthusiastically. We don't expect you to draw like most Russians. And while we do occasionally host professional artists, we see a lot more beginners.
Is there a model?
Yes, there is a model, and they are paid to pose with your suggested donations.
Is the model nude?
Yes.
Is the model male or female?
We have both female and male models.
Do you announce your models in advance?
Yes, we do.
Can I model at Drawing with Russians?
Yes, please attend one event, and ask to pose in person.
How long is drawing?
Drawing is typically 2 hours with breaks every 20 minutes. We start with short 1 minute poses, and finish with longer 20 minute ones.
Do I need to bring paper and crayons?
Yes, please bring your own supplies. That said, we will have some supplies available for those that come without for any reason.
What kind of supplies do you recommend?
We recommend a drawing pad, pencils or charcoal.
Can I bring and use paint?
You can do watercolor. We cannot accommodate other permanently messy materials, such as oils.
Can I talk during drawing?
Absolutely not. Drawing is quiet. No whispering. Please wait for a break to talk to others.
Can I just observe drawing?
No, you must draw.
Do I need to be vaccinated against COVID-19?
We won't check. That said, if you aren't vaccinated you either have a medical reason, or are out of your mind.
Will I have to wear a mask during drawing?
No, you will not need to wear a mask, and expect most people to not be wearing one. The building does require wearing a mask in common areas, including the elevator.
Where is drawing?
Drawing is in Hudson Yards, Manhattan, New York, at the organizer's home and art studio. Invitations include all the location details.
How much does drawing cost?
We suggest a $20 donation directly to the model. If a lot of people show up, we put some of the money towards alcohol.
How many people do usually come to drawing?
We have had the pleasure of hosting 10-25 people. Typically, half stay for the after-party.
How can I pay?
Bring $20 cash, or the model will provide you with their Venmo information during the event.
Do I need to RSVP?
Yes, we ask you to please RSVP by replying to the invitation e-mail. You must RSVP or decline to stay on the list.
What is the drawing vibe?
Drawing is quiet. The vibe is chill. The breaks are social. The after party is bohemian.
Should I also bring alcohol?
Yes, you should bring a bottle of something. In Russia we never go to a house empty-handed. We enjoy socializing during breaks, and drawing is followed by an after-party. We'd rather give all the money to the model, too, and not spend it on booze.
Can I bring a friend?
Yes, and tell them to bring a bottle of wine.
Are MFA students allowed?
Yes, as long as they are young and innocent.
I'm an art or particle physics student in New York. Will I run into one of my professors?
Drawing with Russians does not grade on attendance, and thus cannot guarantee it.
Is this a "drink and draw"?
It certainly looks like one, but we don't like that name.
Is there music?
Yes, we generally play meditative Russian electronic music via SoundCloud, and an occasional classic from the 90s.
Can I still come if I'm late?
Yes you can. No need to warn us. Please walk in quietly, and wait for a break to setup.
Do I have to take my shoes off?
No, you may keep your shoes on if the weather is dry.
Do you have an Instagram?
Yes, please follow @artdblockdotorg.
When did "Drawing with Russians" start?
The event became a regular affair in early post-isolation phase of the 2021 pandemic.
Do you make a unique flyer for every Drawing with Russians occurrence?
Yes. You can also buy them as 1/1 NFTs on Rarible. The first one sold to a major collector. Ape in.
Can I take photos during drawing?
You cannot take photos during a pose without prior agreement with the model.
Is there enough light, or a view?
The loft has bright lights, 10 windows, and a largely unobstructed view of the Empire State building, including from the bathtub.
Is there food?
Absolutely not. This is Drawing with Russians in Manhattan, not a buffet show at Tatiana in Brighton Beach.
Can I bring food?
We'd rather you not. You could order pizza for everyone after drawing if you really want to.
Is there a liquor store where I can buy wine?
Just like in Russia, there is a liquor store within half a block in every direction.
Is there a grocery store where I can buy beer?
Unlike Russia, there's a grocery store across the street, and a deli within half a block in both directions. If you go to the grocery store, please ask whether the person standing in line in front of you likes to draw.
Is there a place to smoke?
The building is non-smoking, including the roof. You'll need to take cigarettes downstairs.
Is there a bathroom?
Yes, however you may find it lacks similar privacy features compared to typical bathrooms.
Can I just come to the after-party?
We really insist that you please come to drawing, first.
What happens at the after-party?
Drawing is followed by a typical Manhattan loft party. That said, please be aware that we usually gather a crowd that is a lot more bohemian and open-minded than at a typical loft party. ðŸ˜ˆ
