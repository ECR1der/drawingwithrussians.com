Frequently Asked Questions

What is Drawing with Russians?
Drawing with Russians is a non-instructed figure drawing class loosely affiliated with Russians, followed by an after-party.
What happens at figure drawing with Russians during drawing?
We chit-chat, have a drink, and draw a model in 20 minute intervals.
Can I come to Drawing with Russians even though I am not Russian?
Yes, you don't need to be Russian to come, but you must plan to draw.
Do I need a referral to come to Drawing with Russians?
Yes, we grow through word of mouth. Please tell us who referred you when subscribing to the list.
I am a terrible noob at drawing. Should I still come?
Please do. We don't expect you to draw like most Russians. And while we do occasionally see professional artists, we see a lot more beginners.
Is there a model?
Yes, there is a model and they are paid to pose.
Is the model nude?
Yes.
Is the model male or female?
We have both female and male models.
How long is drawing?
Drawing is typically 2 hours with breaks every 20 minutes. We start with short 1 minute poses, and finish with longer 20 minute ones.
Do I need to bring paper and crayons?
Yes, please bring your own supplies. That said, we will have some supplies available for those that come without for any reason.
What kind of supplies do you recommend?
We recommend a drawing pad, pencils or charcoal.
Can I bring and use paint?
You can do watercolor. We cannot accommodate other permanently messy materials, such as oils.
Can I talk during drawing?
Absolutely not. Drawing is quiet. No whispering. Please wait for a break to talk to others.
Can I just observe drawing?
No, you must draw.
Do I need to be vaccinated against COVID-19?
We won't check. That said, if you aren't vaccinated you either have a medical reason, or are out of your mind.
Will I have to wear a mask during drawing?
No, you will not need to wear a mask, and expect most people to not be wearing one. The building does require wearing a mask in common areas, including the elevator.
When is the next drawing?
Please subscribe to our mailing list and we'll send you an invitation about a week in advance.
How often do you organize drawing?
We typically organize drawing once a month.
Where is drawing?
Drawing is in Hudson Yards, New York, at the organizer's home and art studio. Invitations include all the location details.
How much does drawing cost?
We suggest a $20 donation directly to the model. If a lot of people show up, we put some of the money towards alcohol.
Do you take Venmo?
Yes we do, usually directly to the model who will provide their payment information.
Do I need to RSVP?
Yes, we ask you to please RSVP by replying to the invitation e-mail.
Should I also bring alcohol?
Yes you should bring a bottle of something. We enjoy socializing during breaks, and drawing is followed by an after-party. We'd rather give all the money to the model, too, and not spend it on booze.
Can I bring a friend?
Yes, and tell them to bring a bottle of wine.
Is this a "drink and draw"?
It certainly looks like one, but we don't like that name.
Is there music?
Yes, we generally play meditative Russian electronic music via SoundCloud.
Can I still come if I'm late?
Yes you can. No need to warn us. Please walk in quietly, and wait for a break to setup.
Do you have an Instagram?
Yes, please follow @artdblockdotorg.
Why is this called "Drawing with Russians"?
The host was born in Moscow, and claims to have lived many different lives.
Can I take photos?
You cannot take photos during a pose without prior agreement with the model.
Is there enough light or a view?
The loft has bright lights, 10 windows, and a largely unobstructed view of the Empire State building, including from the bathtub.
Is there food?
Absolutely not. This is Drawing with Russians in Manhattan, not a buffet show at Tatiana in Brighton Beach.
Can I bring food?
We'd rather you not. You could order pizza for everyone after drawing if you really want to.
Is there a place to smoke?
The building is non-smoking, including the roof. You'll need to take cigarettes downstairs.
Is there a bathroom?
Yes, however you may find it lacks similar privacy features compared to typical bathrooms.
Can I just come to the after-party?
We really insist that you please come to drawing first.
What happens at the after-party?
